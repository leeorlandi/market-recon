# Player Classification Taxonomy

Used by Market Recon (`/market-recon`) to consistently categorize vendors.

---

## Tier 1 — Direct Competitors
Build the same core capability. Compete for the same budget.

**Criteria:**
- Same primary use case
- Same buyer persona
- Substitute-able in a vendor evaluation

**Research priority:** Highest. Moat signals, pricing, recent velocity.

---

## Tier 2 — Adjacent Players
Partial capability overlap. Could expand into direct competition, or could be a partner.

**Criteria:**
- Shared buyer, different primary use case
- Or same use case, different buyer (e.g., SMB vs. Enterprise)
- Often appear in the same shortlists but not head-to-head

**Research priority:** Medium. Watch for expansion signals.

---

## Tier 3 — Infrastructure / Platform Layer
What competitors are built on. Often invisible to buyers.

**Criteria:**
- Data providers, enrichment APIs, LLM providers, messaging infrastructure
- Show up in competitors' tech stacks (BuiltWith, job postings, docs)

**Research priority:** Medium. These are often better procurement targets than competitors.

---

## Tier 4 — Pay-to-Play
Vendors where buying/partnering is more strategically sound than competing.

**Criteria:**
- Entrenched in the buyer's existing stack
- Network effect moat that's not economically rational to replicate
- Complementary, not substitutable

**Research priority:** Low on competitive threat, high on partnership/procurement angle.
